# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are students and passionate language learners. They use the product to quickly estimate their current vocabulary knowledge and make practical reading decisions before starting a new text.

## Product Purpose

This product provides a sound estimation of a user's vocabulary. Based on that estimation, it should:

1. estimate book difficulty before reading,
2. support guided reading by identifying likely unknown words and surfacing them as targeted learning prompts.

Success means users can choose reading material with better confidence and reduce friction during reading by focusing attention on the words most likely to block comprehension.

## Brand Personality

concise, effective, informative

## Anti-references

The product must not feel like it is trying to outdo itself with unnecessary flourish, excessive novelty, or over-engineered interaction patterns. At its core, it should remain a reader experience with algorithmic assistance, not a flashy showcase.

## Design Principles

1. Keep interaction direct and low-friction, users should get from input to useful reading guidance quickly.
2. Make algorithmic output legible, every estimate should be easy to interpret and act on.
3. Prioritize reading utility over visual spectacle, interface choices must support comprehension and flow.
4. Keep progressive depth, show essential information first and reveal detail when it adds reading value.
5. Preserve trust through consistency, identical user actions should produce predictable behavior and stable output framing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Follow general accessibility best practices:

- maintain readable contrast and typography,
- support keyboard navigation for core workflows,
- avoid motion-heavy interactions and respect reduced-motion expectations,
- use clear labels and concise copy for comprehension across proficiency levels.
